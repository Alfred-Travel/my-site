Layover Risk Scoring for Multi-City Trips

Key Logistical Takeaways

  • Connection time alone is not a safety signal.
  • Immigration, terminal transfer, and delay profile should be scored together.
  • Risk scoring improves reliability across the whole itinerary.

Travelers often optimize for short total journey time and accidentally create high-failure itineraries. A 75-minute transfer can be fine in one airport and dangerous in another.

Simple Risk Model

  • Low: same terminal, low border friction, stable on-time profile.
  • Medium: one transfer variable is uncertain.
  • High: border + terminal change + narrow buffer.

Why It Matters

One missed connection can break hotel check-ins, paid activities, and onward routes. Alfred validates transfer chains so your multi-city plan remains executable.
