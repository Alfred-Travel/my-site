City Pass vs Pay-As-You-Go: The Trip Math Most People Skip

Key Logistical Takeaways

  • Value depends on achievable entries, not listed entries.
  • Queue and transit overhead reduce real pass utility.
  • Math should use your actual day structure.

City passes look like instant savings, but only if your route can realistically fit the included sites. Many travelers overestimate how many timed attractions can be completed in one day.

Compute Realizable Value

Count only attractions that match your location flow, opening hours, and queue risk. If two included sites sit in opposite districts with long transfer times, treat one as optional.

Price the Convenience Layer

Skip-the-line and bundled transit may still justify a pass even with modest direct savings. The right comparison includes time saved and planning simplicity, not ticket totals only.

Use Itinerary Math, Not Marketing Math

Alfred can evaluate both options against your schedule blocks, helping you choose a pass when it genuinely improves trip execution.
